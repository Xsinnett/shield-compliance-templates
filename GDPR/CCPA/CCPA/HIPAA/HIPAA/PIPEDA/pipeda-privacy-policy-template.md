## 3. What is Personal Information?

Personal information is any information about an identifiable 
individual, including but not limited to:

- Name
- Home address and email address
- Age and date of birth
- ID numbers
- Income and financial information
- Ethnic origin
- Blood type and medical records
- Opinions, evaluations, and social status
- Employee files and disciplinary actions
- Credit records and loan records

Business contact information such as your name, title, business 
address, and business phone number used strictly for business 
communications is not considered personal information under PIPEDA.

## 4. The Ten Privacy Principles

Our privacy practices are based on the ten fair information principles 
established under PIPEDA:

### Principle 1 — Accountability
We are responsible for the personal information under our control. 
We have designated a Privacy Officer who is accountable for our 
compliance with these principles.

### Principle 2 — Identifying Purposes
We identify the purposes for which personal information is collected 
at or before the time of collection.

### Principle 3 — Consent
We obtain your knowledge and consent for the collection, use, or 
disclosure of personal information, except where inappropriate or 
not required by law.

### Principle 4 — Limiting Collection
We limit the collection of personal information to that which is 
necessary for the identified purposes.

### Principle 5 — Limiting Use, Disclosure, and Retention
We use or disclose personal information only for the purposes for 
which it was collected, except with your consent or as required by 
law. We retain personal information only as long as necessary.

### Principle 6 — Accuracy
We keep personal information as accurate, complete, and up to date 
as necessary for the purposes for which it is to be used.

### Principle 7 — Safeguards
We protect personal information with security safeguards appropriate 
to the sensitivity of the information.

### Principle 8 — Openness
We make our privacy policies and practices readily available to you.

### Principle 9 — Individual Access
Upon request, we inform you of the existence, use, and disclosure of 
your personal information and give you access to that information.

### Principle 10 — Challenging Compliance
You may challenge our compliance with these principles by contacting 
our Privacy Officer.
