 PIPEDA Compliance Checklist

*Prepared by Shield Compliance | shieldcompliance.carrd.co*  
*This checklist is for informational purposes only and does not 
constitute legal advice.*

---

## 1. Does PIPEDA Apply to You?
- [ ] Your organization is a private sector organization
- [ ] You collect, use, or disclose personal information in the 
      course of commercial activities
- [ ] You operate in a province without substantially similar 
      privacy legislation (Alberta, British Columbia, and Quebec 
      have their own legislation — check if PIPEDA still applies 
      to your interprovincial or international activities)

---

## 2. Accountability (Principle 1)
- [ ] Designated a Privacy Officer responsible for PIPEDA compliance
- [ ] Privacy Officer contact information is publicly available
- [ ] Privacy policies and procedures are documented in writing
- [ ] All staff are trained on privacy responsibilities
- [ ] Contracts with third parties require them to protect personal 
      information to PIPEDA standards
- [ ] Process in place to receive and respond to privacy complaints

## 3. Identifying Purposes (Principle 2)
- [ ] Documented the purpose for collecting each type of personal 
      information
- [ ] Purposes are communicated to individuals at or before 
      point of collection
- [ ] New purposes identified and consent obtained before using 
      information for a new purpose
- [ ] Purposes are limited to what a reasonable person would 
      consider appropriate

## 4. Consent (Principle 3)
- [ ] Obtaining meaningful consent before or at time of collection
- [ ] Consent requests are written in plain language
- [ ] Individuals can easily withdraw consent at any time
- [ ] Process in place to honor consent withdrawals promptly
- [ ] Not requiring consent beyond what is necessary as a condition 
      of service
- [ ] Implied consent used only where appropriate for less sensitive 
      information
- [ ] Express consent obtained for sensitive personal information

## 5. Limiting Collection (Principle 4)
- [ ] Only collecting personal information necessary for 
      identified purposes
- [ ] Not collecting personal information indiscriminately
- [ ] Collection methods are fair and lawful
- [ ] Data inventory maintained documenting what is collected and why

## 6. Limiting Use, Disclosure & Retention (Principle 5)
- [ ] Personal information used only for purposes it was collected for
- [ ] Personal information not disclosed to third parties without 
      consent except as required by law
- [ ] Retention schedule defined for each type of personal information
- [ ] Personal information destroyed or anonymized when no longer needed
- [ ] Records of personal information disposal maintained

## 7. Accuracy (Principle 6)
- [ ] Process in place to keep personal information accurate 
      and up to date
- [ ] Personal information updated when individuals request corrections
- [ ] Corrected information shared with third parties where necessary
- [ ] Unnecessary or outdated information removed or corrected

## 8. Safeguards (Principle 7)
- [ ] Security safeguards appropriate to sensitivity of information
- [ ] Physical safeguards in place (locked files, restricted access)
- [ ] Technical safeguards in place (encryption, firewalls, passwords)
- [ ] Organizational safeguards in place (training, policies, 
      need-to-know access)
- [ ] Third parties handling personal information provide 
      comparable protection
- [ ] Process in place to detect, contain, and report breaches
- [ ] Individuals notified of breaches that pose a real risk of 
      significant harm
- [ ] Privacy Commissioner notified of qualifying breaches
- [ ] Breach records maintained for 24 months

## 9. Openness (Principle 8)
- [ ] Privacy policy is publicly available and easy to find
- [ ] Privacy policy written in plain language
- [ ] Privacy policy explains what information is collected and why
- [ ] Privacy policy explains how to contact the Privacy Officer
- [ ] Privacy policy explains how to access personal information 
      or file a complaint
- [ ] Privacy policy updated within the last 12 months

## 10. Individual Access (Principle 9)
- [ ] Process in place to respond to access requests within 30 days
- [ ] Able to inform individuals what personal information is held
- [ ] Able to explain how personal information has been used
- [ ] Able to identify third parties personal information 
      has been disclosed to
- [ ] Process in place to correct or annotate inaccurate information
- [ ] Challenging access requests only where permitted by PIPEDA
- [ ] Not charging excessive fees for access requests

## 11. Challenging Compliance (Principle 10)
- [ ] Process in place to receive and investigate privacy complaints
- [ ] Complaints investigated by or reported to Privacy Officer
- [ ] Complainants informed of outcome of their complaint
- [ ] Procedures amended where complaints reveal areas for improvement
- [ ] Individuals informed of their right to complain to the 
      Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada
