## 1. Does HIPAA Apply to You?
- [ ] You are a Covered Entity (health plan, health care clearinghouse, 
      or health care provider that transmits health information 
      electronically)
- [ ] You are a Business Associate (a person or entity that performs 
      services for a Covered Entity involving the use or disclosure 
      of PHI)
- [ ] You have identified all Business Associates and have signed 
      Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with each one

---

## 2. Privacy Rule Compliance
- [ ] Designated a Privacy Officer responsible for HIPAA compliance
- [ ] Developed and implemented a HIPAA Privacy Policy
- [ ] Privacy Policy is available to patients and posted on website
- [ ] Privacy Policy reviewed and updated within the last 12 months
- [ ] Procedures in place for patients to exercise their rights
- [ ] Process in place to respond to patient rights requests 
      within 30 days
- [ ] Only using or disclosing the minimum necessary PHI for each purpose
- [ ] Tracking all disclosures of PHI not related to treatment, 
      payment, or operations
- [ ] Obtaining written authorization for uses not permitted by HIPAA
- [ ] Not retaliating against individuals who file complaints

## 3. Security Rule Compliance

### Administrative Safeguards
- [ ] Conducted and documented a security risk assessment
- [ ] Implemented a risk management plan to address identified risks
- [ ] Designated a Security Officer responsible for security policies
- [ ] Implemented procedures to authorize access to PHI
- [ ] Workforce training on HIPAA security requirements completed
- [ ] Sanctions policy in place for workforce members who violate 
      security policies
- [ ] Regular review of system activity logs and audit reports

### Physical Safeguards
- [ ] Physical access to facilities storing PHI is controlled
- [ ] Workstations accessing PHI are in secure locations
- [ ] Workstation screens not visible to unauthorized individuals
- [ ] Procedures for secure disposal of PHI and hardware
- [ ] Device and media controls in place for PHI on portable devices

### Technical Safeguards
- [ ] Unique user IDs assigned to each workforce member
- [ ] Automatic logoff implemented on systems containing PHI
- [ ] Encryption of PHI in transit (e.g. SSL/TLS)
- [ ] Encryption of PHI at rest where appropriate
- [ ] Audit controls in place to record system activity
- [ ] Integrity controls to ensure PHI is not improperly altered
- [ ] Transmission security controls in place

## 4. Breach Notification Rule
- [ ] Process in place to identify and respond to breaches of PHI
- [ ] Able to notify affected individuals within 60 days of 
      discovering a breach
- [ ] Able to notify HHS Secretary of breaches
- [ ] Able to notify media for breaches affecting 500+ individuals 
      in a state
- [ ] Maintaining a log of all breaches affecting fewer than 
      500 individuals
- [ ] Breach notification procedures tested and documented

## 5. Business Associate Agreements
- [ ] Identified all Business Associates who handle PHI
- [ ] Signed BAAs in place with all Business Associates
- [ ] BAAs require Business Associates to report breaches
- [ ] BAAs require Business Associates to protect PHI per HIPAA
- [ ] BAAs reviewed and updated when relationships change

## 6. Patient Rights
- [ ] Process in place for patients to access their PHI
- [ ] Process in place for patients to request amendments to their PHI
- [ ] Process in place for patients to request restrictions on use 
      of their PHI
- [ ] Process in place for patients to request confidential 
      communications
- [ ] Process in place for patients to request an accounting 
      of disclosures
- [ ] Process in place to handle complaints about privacy practices

## 7. Workforce Training
- [ ] All workforce members trained on HIPAA Privacy Rule
- [ ] All workforce members trained on HIPAA Security Rule
- [ ] Training completed at hire and annually thereafter
- [ ] Training records maintained for at least 6 years
- [ ] Additional training provided when policies change

## 8. Documentation & Record Keeping
- [ ] All HIPAA policies and procedures documented in writing
- [ ] Documentation retained for a minimum of 6 years
- [ ] Security risk assessments documented and retained
- [ ] Training records maintained
- [ ] Breach logs maintained
- [ ] BAAs retained for 6 years after termination
